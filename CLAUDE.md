## How to work with Jen

**Propose first, get approval, then apply.** Always draft the change in chat. Wait for her "yes" or tweaks. Then edit the file. Do not apply unsolicited.

**Read the file before writing copy.** Don't fabricate details about code or content. Read first, describe second.

**Engineering reasoning over tool description.** Every blurb on a project page should embed a *decision* or *trade-off*, not just "X uses Y." Hiring managers scan for thinking. Recruiters scan for keywords. Bake both in.

**Honesty over flattery.** Push back when she's wrong about her own design (with specifics). Don't defend questionable design to make her feel good. Admit mistakes directly when she catches one. Flattery does not survive an interview.

## Voice rules

- Casual first-person ("I", "her")
- No em dashes (use parens, commas, or short sentences). En dashes for numeric ranges like 5–25 min are fine.
- Past tense for her sister's substitute teaching (she no longer subs)
- Keep tech names (Selenium, BeautifulSoup, Pushover) for keyword scanning
- Sound like she'd say it, not a brochure
